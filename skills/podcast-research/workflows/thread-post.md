# Workflow — Thread (Post-Interview)

Use this workflow when writing a thread to publish *after* recording, once
the episode is live or scheduled. Goal: synthesize the conversation into the
2–3 things that actually landed, surface the most quotable or surprising moment,
and drive listens through specificity.

Load `style/voice.md` and `style/examples.md` before writing anything.

---

## Inputs Required

At least one of:
- Transcript or timestamped notes from the episode
- Bullet points of key moments provided by the host
- Host's memory of what surprised them or stuck with them

Optional but useful:
- Pre-interview data snapshot (from `workflows/data-snapshot.md`) — for comparison
- Guest diligence file — to verify claims made in conversation

---

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1 — Extract the 3 Most Valuable Moments
From the transcript or notes, identify:

1. **The Reveal** — Something the guest said that complicated or contradicted
   the obvious read of their project. (e.g., "Demand for privacy wasn't what
   we expected.")

2. **The Mechanism** — A specific technical or strategic explanation that
   most coverage won't include. (e.g., "Hidden order features prevent order hunting.")

3. **The Human Moment** — A quote, admission, or personality detail that
   made this conversation feel like more than a product pitch.
   (e.g., "We even got a smile or two out of Alex.")

If you can't identify all three, the thread will be thin. Ask the host before writing.

### Step 2 — Identify Partner / Context Tweet (if applicable)
If the episode was produced in partnership with a sponsor or at a conference,
tweet 2 handles that. Keep it warm and specific — name people and places,
not just logos.

### Step 3 — Compare Pre vs. Post Data (if data snapshot exists)
If a pre-interview snapshot was taken with `workflows/data-snapshot.md`,
re-pull the same metrics and note what changed between recording and publish.

Use this comparison in tweet 3 or 4 if the delta is material and relevant
to what was discussed. Format:

```
At the time of recording: [metric] was [value] — [source, date]
At publish: [metric] is now [value] — [source, date]
[One sentence on what this means in context of what the guest said]
```

Only include this if the change is meaningful. Don't force a comparison
just because the numbers changed.

### Step 4 — Write the Thread

**Standard structure (6 tweets):**

```
[1/6] Hook — frame the guest's significance + embed media.
      Use the counter-narrative if one exists (reframe "overnight success",
      challenge the bullish consensus, surface what's not obvious).

[2/6] Partner acknowledgment (if applicable) OR the episode's central theme.
      If no partner, use this tweet to set up the tension the conversation
      explored.

[3/6] The Mechanism — the most technically specific takeaway.
      Name protocols, name the mechanism, don't over-explain.

[4/6] The Reveal — the thing that surprised you or complicated the obvious read.
      This is often the most re-shareable tweet.

[5/6] The Human Moment OR the builder philosophy.
      The quote or the candor that made this feel like a real conversation.

[6/6] Personal reflection + CTA.
      Warm close. Standard: "I hope you enjoy this interview as much as I did!"
      Link to episode.
```

### Step 5 — Apply Voice Rules
Run through `style/voice.md` checklist:
- [ ] [N/Total] numbering on every tweet
- [ ] No hashtags
- [ ] No emojis
- [ ] One connector verb per tweet, not repeated
- [ ] Guest referred to by first name or "He" after tweet 1 — not "the guest"
- [ ] At most one direct quote, formatted as `[Name]: "[Quote]"`
- [ ] Close is personal, not promotional

---

## Output Format

Plain numbered tweets, ready to copy-paste. No surrounding commentary.

```
[1/6] ...

[2/6] ...

[3/6] ...

[4/6] ...

[5/6] ...

[6/6] ...
```

If a pre/post data comparison was included, append:

```
---
Pre-recording snapshot: [Date] — [Metric]: [Value]
Post-recording (publish date): [Metric]: [Value]
Source: [Platform]
```
