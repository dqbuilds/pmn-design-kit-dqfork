# Tier 1 — Crypto-Native News Outlets

Treat these as primary sources for on-chain events, protocol news, regulatory
developments, token mechanics, and market structure. Always prefer over Tier 3
(traditional financial media). Read use-case notes carefully — outlet quality
varies by topic area.

---

## Outlets

### The Block
**Best for:** Institutional data, funding rounds, M&A, regulatory tracking,
exchange data, research reports. Has a dedicated data dashboard and funding
tracker that are among the most reliable in the space.
**Use with caution:** Opinion pieces and market commentary are lower signal
than their data/research output. Distinguish between The Block News and
The Block Research.
**Corroborate when:** Citing funding round figures — cross-check with
Crunchbase or company announcements.

### CoinDesk
**Best for:** Breaking regulatory news, policy developments, market structure,
institutional adoption, long-form features. Consensus conference coverage is
authoritative.
**Use with caution:** Owned by Bullish (a crypto exchange) — disclose this
context when covering exchange-related topics. Volume of output means quality
varies by reporter.
**Corroborate when:** Covering stories with direct competitive relevance to
Bullish or its affiliates.

### Blockworks
**Best for:** Institutional macro perspective, trading desk insights, research
arm (Blockworks Research) for deep quantitative analysis, podcast transcripts
from Empire and On the Margin.
**Use with caution:** Leans bullish in editorial framing. Good for institutional
narrative, weaker on adversarial/critical takes.
**Corroborate when:** Any price or market prediction — treat as perspective,
not forecast.

### The Defiant
**Best for:** DeFi-native coverage — protocol mechanics, governance votes,
liquidity analysis, DEX data. Best outlet for understanding the "why" behind
protocol decisions.
**Use with caution:** Smaller team means breaking news can lag. Strongest on
analysis, not always first on facts.
**Corroborate when:** Any claim about protocol TVL or governance outcomes —
verify directly on DefiLlama or Snapshot/Tally.

### Unchained / Laura Shin
**Best for:** Deep investigative journalism, long-form narrative, DeFi history,
founder interviews. Best for understanding the human/political story behind
crypto events.
**Use with caution:** Monthly cadence means coverage lags breaking news.
**Corroborate when:** Historical claims about early DeFi — verify dates and
figures against on-chain data.

### Decrypt
**Best for:** Consumer crypto, NFTs, culture, gaming, accessible explainers,
mainstream adoption stories. Best for understanding how non-technical audiences
encounter crypto.
**Use with caution:** Less rigorous on technical claims than The Block or
Blockworks Research. Stronger on narrative than data.
**Corroborate when:** Any technical protocol claim — verify against docs or
Tier 2 data sources.

### BeInCrypto
**Best for:** Global and emerging markets coverage, altcoin research, regional
regulatory developments (LATAM, SEA, MENA), retail sentiment.
**Use with caution:** Quality varies significantly by region/reporter. Treat
as directional signal, not authoritative source, unless citing a specific
well-sourced piece.
**Corroborate when:** Any statistical claim — always find the original source.

### TBPN (The Breakdown / NLW)
**Best for:** Macro-crypto narrative, daily news synthesis, institutional
context, geopolitical framing of crypto events. Nathaniel Whittemore is
excellent at framing why a story matters, not just what happened.
**Use with caution:** Commentary and analysis, not primary reporting. Never
cite TBPN as the source of a fact — cite the underlying event/document.
**Corroborate when:** Always. Use as context layer, not citation.

### Protos
**Best for:** Critical and skeptical takes, investigative reporting on bad
actors, exchange malfeasance, stablecoin risks, scam exposure. Best source
for the adversarial angle on any bullish narrative.
**Use with caution:** Explicitly skeptical editorial stance — balance with
other sources for complete picture.
**Corroborate when:** Covering projects or founders Protos has written about —
check if their criticism is substantiated or contested.

### Cointelegraph
**Best for:** Broad coverage, corroboration of events already reported
elsewhere, international crypto news.
**Use with caution:** High volume, variable quality. Known for SEO-driven
content alongside legitimate reporting. Do not use as a sole source.
**Corroborate when:** Always treat as secondary confirmation, not primary.

## Podcast Discovery

### Podscan — podscan.fm
**Best for:** Finding recent podcast appearances by a guest across all major shows,
surfacing trending podcast topics in crypto, identifying what narratives are
being discussed on competing shows, and pulling specific episode highlights or
quotes related to a topic.
**Use for:** Guest research (Step 3 in `workflows/guest-diligence.md`) — search
the guest's name to find recent appearances and what they said elsewhere before
the interview. Also use when scoping a segment topic to see what angles have
already been covered on other shows.
**How to use:** Search by guest name, topic keyword, or episode title. Filter by
date range to surface only recent appearances (last 90 days for fast-moving topics).
**Use with caution:** Transcript accuracy varies by episode. Treat pulled quotes
as directional — verify exact wording against the original audio before citing
on-air.
**Corroborate when:** Attributing a specific claim to a guest based on a
Podscan transcript — always confirm the quote exists in the episode.

## When to Use Tier 3 Instead

Reach for Bloomberg, WSJ, or Financial Times when:
- The story is primarily a TradFi institution making a crypto move
  (their internal sourcing will be superior)
- You need macro economic context (Fed rates, credit markets, institutional flows)
- The crypto outlet coverage is clearly downstream of a TradFi original report

Always note in citations when a Tier 1 crypto outlet is itself citing a
Tier 3 source — trace back to the original.
