# Thread Examples — Annotated

Real threads from @imyoungsparks. Use these as the primary style reference
when generating thread output. The annotations explain *why* each choice works —
read them before writing, not after.

---

## Example A — Post-Interview Thread (zkSync / Matter Labs)
*Oct 8, 2025 | Guest: @gluk64, Co-Founder & CEO of @the_matter_labs*

---

**[1/6]** I had a lovely podcast yesterday with @gluk64 Co-Founder and CEO
of @the_matter_labs. We discussed the past, present, and future states of
privacy onchain, scaling Ethereum, and the role @zksync plays in building
the incorruptible economy

[embedded: The Block article link]

> **What works:** Opens with a personal, warm framing ("lovely podcast") rather
> than a hype claim. The three-topic list in the hook gives readers a reason to
> continue. The incorruptible economy phrase is specific and memorable — it's the
> guest's language, adopted naturally. Includes embedded media immediately.

---

**[2/6]** We covered a wide range of topics like the role the cypherpunk ethos
plays in the onchain future and how Matter Labs is bringing this vision to
fruition. Alex had many extremely profound takes. He spoke on the importance
of interdisciplinary skillsets needed for innovation.

> **What works:** Widens the frame of the conversation before narrowing to
> specifics. The admiration ("extremely profound takes") is genuine, not hyperbolic.
> Introduces a non-obvious theme (interdisciplinary skillsets) that signals depth.

---

**[3/6]** There was a huge reveal in the latter portion of the podcast on the
Atlas upgrade for zkSync which mitigates issues of fragmented liquidity,
real-time finality of ZKPs, and privacy preservation.

> **What works:** "Huge reveal" earns the word because it's the Atlas upgrade —
> specific, verifiable, not generic hype. Three concrete technical problems named
> directly without over-explaining. Respects the audience's technical literacy.

---

**[4/6]** Alex expounded on what this means for user experience onchain.
Additionally, he shared struggles of a builder on the bleeding edge being in
the space for +8 years and navigating gut wrenching volatility.

> **What works:** Pivots from technical to human. "Gut wrenching volatility" is
> vivid and honest — not sanitized. The +8 years grounds the experience claim
> with a specific number. Connector "expounded on" suits the weight of the topic.

---

**[5/6]** Though there were many technical and potent quotes throughout the
conversation one specifically stuck out to me. It was something along the lines
of freedom enables progress and progress enables innovation.

> **What works:** Signals a highlight moment without formal quote marks — "along
> the lines of" is honest framing that acknowledges paraphrase. The quote itself
> is worth surfacing: it's a thesis, not a data point.

---

**[6/6]** I was often left in awe by his passion, devotion, and conviction for
his vision of the future of Ethereum. Although the conversation was quite
serious we even got a smile or two out of Alex.

I hope you enjoy listening to his profound insights as much as I did!

> **What works:** The human detail ("got a smile or two out of Alex") makes the
> close feel real, not templated. The CTA is warm and personal. The sentence
> structure is simple — two short sentences, then the invitation.

## Example B — Post-Interview Thread (Aster DEX / Leonard)
*Oct 27, 2025 | Guest: @Leonard_Aster, Founder of @Aster_DEX*

---

**[1/6]** Though characterized an an 'overnight success' I had a chance to sit
down with @Leonard_Aster and let him illuminate the long road that fueled the
explosive growth @Aster_DEX, the renaissance of the Perp DEX meta, and
insights of a DeFi builder

[embedded: The Block article]

> **What works:** Opens by immediately reframing the guest's narrative —
> "overnight success" is in quotes, signaling you're going to complicate that.
> Three-part payoff (growth story / meta context / builder lens) in one tweet.

---

**[2/6]** Firstly, before I dive into the interview. I'd like to thank our
partners @BNBCHAIN for making this interview possible. We had a blast at the
Hanok House and this wouldn't have been possible without our amazing friends
like @cydeologie, @sky3lo, @cain_bnb, and many others!

> **What works:** Partner acknowledgment handled warmly and specifically — names
> people, references the venue, genuine energy. Not a dry sponsorship mention.
> Second tweet is the right placement so it doesn't eat the hook.

---

**[3/6]** The growth in August/September of Aster was something that came from
the team's years of insights in the space. He outlined predecessors like
@dYdX, @GMX_IO, @HyperliquidX and how they drove innovations in onchain
trading and hype cycles.

> **What works:** Specific timeframe (Aug/Sep) makes the claim verifiable.
> Naming predecessors dYdX, GMX, Hyperliquid signals serious market structure
> knowledge — not just Aster promotion. "Hype cycles" is honest framing.

---

**[4/6]** Leonard spoke to the importance of privacy preservation in certain
respects and why hidden order features prevent order hunting. This was discussed
in a public manner to which Aster quickly responded to community demand.

> **What works:** Technical mechanism (hidden order features → prevents order
> hunting) explained in one sentence without over-simplifying. The community
> responsiveness detail adds a builder credibility angle.

---

**[5/6]** He noted demand for privacy preservation on Aster wasn't what the
team initially expected. As a builder he wants to build the features that the
community desires rather than falling in the trap of building what he thinks
is cool.

> **What works:** A counter-expectation reveal — the community wanted something
> the team didn't anticipate. This is the kind of candor that makes a thread
> worth reading. The builder philosophy in the second sentence is quotable.

---

**[6/6]** Leonard was such a great guest and really expressed his gratitude to
the Aster community for making the team and product much better. Team is still
shipping and so much came out after Korea Blockchain Week.

I hope you enjoy this interview as much as I did!

> **What works:** Closes by pointing to continued momentum (post-KBW shipping)
> without specific claims. Gratitude toward the community, not self-promotion.
> Standard CTA close lands naturally.

## Example C — Pre-Interview / Analysis Thread (Graph Algorithms + AI Agents)
*Jan 12, 2026 | Topic: SSSP breakthrough + implications for AI agents onchain*

---

**[1/11]** It's cool seeing issues I learned about in CS getting solved! Though
there isn't much overlap with crypto at times. Directed graphs for payment
channel networks (finding paths with sufficient capacity and low fees, akin to
weighted SSSP) is very relevant in AI agent behaviors!

[embedded: source tweet on SSSP breakthrough]

> **What works:** Opens with personal context (CS background) that earns the
> credibility to make the bridge to crypto. "Though there isn't much overlap
> with crypto at times" is an honest admission that makes the connection feel
> earned, not forced.

---

**[2/11]** A faster algorithm for route discovery in payment channel networks,
when integrated with AI, could enable autonomous agents to execute onchain
and cross-chain transactions with greater speed, efficiency, and adaptability.

> **What works:** Thesis tweet. Clear mechanism (faster routing → agent execution)
> stated simply. Sets up the chain of reasoning to follow.

---

**[3–8/11]** [Each tweet develops one implication: routing optimization,
volatility navigation, network prediction, fee minimization, privacy tools,
graph computation scaling]

> **What works:** One idea per tweet, no stacking. Each builds on the prior
> without restating it. Technical but not impenetrable — uses analogies
> ("intelligent routers", "human-like behavior").

---

**[9/11]** In summary, the potential lies in scaling up graph computations on
sparse directed networks, with the strongest enablers in CS applications like
routing and optimization. For physics and applied math, it's enabling for
simulations and models.

---

**[10/11]** If I were an application it would be worthwhile figuring out how to
tap into this as evidenced by reduced industry participation. These are still
complex tasks that these agents can now abstract away. I see huge UX unlocks if
an app can tap into this.

> **What works:** "If I were an application..." is a signature framing device —
> first-person hypothetical that makes the implications concrete. "I see huge UX
> unlocks" is an opinion clearly attributed to the author.

---

**[11/11]** Overall, this could accelerate the shift toward fully autonomous
economies, where AI agents operate with minimal human intervention, but it also
raises challenges like increased network spam or unintended flash crashes if not
governed properly.

> **What works:** Closes with both the bull and bear case — doesn't just cheerlead.
> "If not governed properly" signals epistemic honesty. Good final tweet because
> it opens a question rather than closing with a declaration.

## Key Patterns Across All Examples

1. **Hook never leads with price or market performance.** Always leads with
   the story, the person, or the idea.

2. **Warmth is consistent but earns its place.** Admiration ("profound",
   "such a great guest") appears once or twice per thread — not every tweet.

3. **Mechanisms are named, not summarized.** "Hidden order features prevent
   order hunting" not "privacy features help DeFi users."

4. **The guest's own framing is honored.** When a guest has a thesis or
   philosophy, it gets its own tweet with clean attribution.

5. **The thread ends looking forward**, not backward — at what's shipping,
   what the question is, or what the listener will find if they tune in.

## Example D — Blended Voice (Santiago Influence)
*Illustrative post-interview thread for the Layer 1 / Santi episode*
*This is a constructed example showing how the two voices merge*

---

**[1/6]** Santiago R. Santos built FDV.gg over a weekend with AI — a tool
that surfaces logical inconsistencies in how investors value crypto vs. the
broader investable universe.

The result? Even the most die-hard bulls can't always explain why Solana
deserves its market cap over Stripe.

[embed episode link]

> **What works:** Opens with the zoom-out move immediately — Santi's tool
> forces the reframe from crypto-vs-crypto to crypto-vs-everything. No hype.
> The second sentence lands the point: the tool *disarms* believers, which
> is more interesting than confirming them. Kelvin's warm, specific framing
> is preserved throughout.

---

**[2/6]** Santi's core position: love the technology. Hate the structures.

He's one of the most active angels in crypto, still heavily allocated —
but comes at every investment from the question of absolute returns, not
relative crypto performance. That distinction matters more than it sounds.

> **What works:** "Love the tech, hate the price" separation stated cleanly
> in two sentences. The contrast ("still heavily allocated — but...") prevents
> this from reading as a bear take and gives it the intellectual honesty that
> makes Santi's framing so hard to dismiss. Mirrors his actual voice.

---

**[3/6]** His structural critique of crypto investing: when a fund's mandate
is crypto-only, they can never acknowledge the opportunity cost of sitting
in crypto. That's not bad faith — it's the principal-agent problem at work.

Crypto VC performance vs. S&P is the question most funds don't want to answer.

> **What works:** Names the structural issue (mandate constraint, principal-agent)
> rather than saying "VCs are biased." Santi's analytical move; Kelvin's clean
> delivery. The final sentence is a question the listener can actually sit with.

---

**[4/6]** On supply curves: Santiago's view is that most people model demand
correctly but systematically underestimate how elastic the supply of blockspace
has become.

Anyone can spin up a chain now. That changes the valuation math for L1s
more than most models account for. (opinion)

> **What works:** Supply/demand framing directly from Santi's framework.
> Opinion labeled explicitly per voice.md. Short, declarative last sentence
> follows a longer setup — rhythm matches both voices.

---

**[5/6]** One moment that stuck with me: he described passing on Terra/LUNA
after a meeting with Do Kwon near Stanford. He played dumb and asked for
basic diligence. Kwon wouldn't furnish it. He did the channel checks anyway.

Both his missed upside and his avoided loss came from the same process.

> **What works:** Specific anecdote with names, location, and outcome. The
> last sentence is the insight — the same methodology produces both types of
> result, which is the honest version of "trust your process." Kelvin's
> admiration for the guest's depth shows without being explicit.

---

**[6/6]** Santiago is building Inversion Capital around a thesis that every
business will eventually move settlement flows to stablecoins — and that the
right entry point is buying the businesses, not the tokens.

Whether or not you agree, this conversation sharpens the question.

I hope you find it as clarifying as I did.

> **What works:** Closes with the guest's generational trade framed in one
> sentence, then an honest CTA — "sharpens the question" rather than "has
> all the answers." The last sentence adapts the standard Kelvin close without
> being formulaic. Leaves the listener curious rather than sold.

## Key Differences Between Examples A–C and Example D

The Santi-influenced voice changes three things:

1. **Hooks interrogate structure, not just content.** Instead of "we discussed X,"
   the hook frames the analytical tension ("even die-hard bulls can't justify...").

2. **Counterarguments are acknowledged, not avoided.** The thread earns its
   convictions by presenting the structural problem clearly rather than
   asserting the conclusion.

3. **Opinion is more present — and labeled.** The Santi voice has sharper
   point of view. The Kelvin voice makes sure it's marked as such.

The warmth, the [N/total] structure, the connector verbs, the personal close —
all of that stays. The intellectual architecture gets bolder.

## Example E — Blended Voice (Haseeb + Sean Influence)
*Illustrative post-interview thread for the Layer 1 EP01 / Haseeb + Sean episode*

---

**[1/6]** Haseeb Qureshi (Dragonfly) and Sean (FinTech Collective) joined the
Layer One pod to talk DeFi scaling, DAT structure, and why the market cycle
you invest in has almost nothing to do with the cycle your investment matures in.

[embed episode link]

> **What works:** Three-part payoff in the hook gives structure without spoiling
> the arguments. "The cycle you invest in has almost nothing to do with the cycle
> your investment matures in" is the Haseeb time-horizon move stated as a hook —
> provocative and specific, not generic. No hype language, no superlatives.

---

**[2/6]** Sean named the regime upfront: a year ago this was a retail-driven
AI/meme cycle. Today it's institutional — stablecoins, tokenization, the
revenue meta. His point: the regime flips faster than a seed investment vests.

Build your thesis for 2029, not for the market you're looking at today.

> **What works:** Sean's regime-naming move executed cleanly. The second sentence
> is the application — translates the observation into an investing principle.
> Kelvin's narrator structure preserved: "Sean named," "His point."

---

**[3/6]** Haseeb's framework on the Balancer hack: calibrate the severity
before reacting. This was an old contract on the third-largest AMM, with pools
that only contained ETH. Real, but not existential.

His call: crypto probably needs a Project Zero equivalent — a funded public
service for responsible vulnerability disclosure, not just individual bug bounties.

> **What works:** "Crown jewel calibration" move from the voice rules — names
> what it is and isn't before making the positive argument. The Project Zero
> proposal is a specific, nameable idea that a listener can look up and think
> about. This is how Haseeb's voice earns its credibility: a concrete proposal
> follows every critique.

---

**[4/6]** The Stream Finance vs. Athena comparison was one of the clearest
frameworks of the episode. Athena: real-time on-chain visibility into reserves
and P&L, no counterparty risk. Stream: opaque off-chain strategy, daily
redemptions, no disclosure, 25% yield.

Haseeb: "You cannot get 25% yield without taking a lot of risk. The only
question is when and how that risk manifests." (opinion)

> **What works:** Mechanism contrast explained in two parallel sentences — no
> jargon required to understand why one held and one didn't. Haseeb's actual
> framing used cleanly, labeled as opinion. The financial transparency principle
> (Athena's transparency = DeFi promise) connects to Sean's "Show the Book" move.

---

**[5/6]** Sean's honest worry: some DAT teams are not equipped for the asset
management side, and the incentive structure is already pointing toward moral
hazard. A 1–2% fee converts what should be wound down into a vehicle that stays
open and takes on risk.

He's seen the writing on the wall for a few of them. This cycle has a Celsius
waiting inside it if teams don't build transparency in from the start.

> **What works:** Sean's "honest worry" move — personal, specific, named. The
> Celsius reference grounds the systemic risk without over-claiming. "He's seen
> the writing" is the field-work credibility signal. This tweet will age well if
> Sean is right; if he's wrong, it's labeled as his worry, not a prediction.

---

**[6/6]** The through-line of the whole conversation: the optimal investing
strategy was never going to hit every winner. Process, time horizon, and regime
awareness matter more than call rate.

Both Haseeb and Sean have thought about this longer than most. Worth the listen.

I hope you take as much from it as I did.

> **What works:** Closes with the synthesis of both voices — Haseeb's optimal
> strategy framing + Sean's process orientation — without attributing each to
> each. The penultimate sentence is spare credentialing. Final close adapts the
> standard Kelvin formula ("as much as I did") without being identical.

## How Haseeb and Sean Differ From Each Other (and When to Use Which)

**Haseeb** is the framework builder. When a topic needs a memorable first-principles
anchor — a single sentence that explains the whole mechanism — reach for his style.
"You can't get 25% yield without taking a lot of risk" is a Haseeb sentence.

**Sean** is the regime watcher and honest worrier. When a topic needs to be
grounded in time and market conditions, or when there's a specific risk that
deserves naming but isn't being named — reach for his style. "I worry that..." is
a Sean sentence.

**Neither replaces Santi.** Santi's zoom-out (compare against the investable
universe, not just crypto) is a different move from Haseeb's time-horizon anchor
and Sean's regime naming. All three can appear in the same thread in different
tweets — they solve different problems.

**All three layer onto Kelvin's base voice:** the warmth, the [N/total] structure,
the connector language, the personal close. The intellectual architecture gets
richer. The narrator's posture doesn't change.

## Example F — Data-Delivery Tweets (Adam Tehc Style)
*Illustrative examples showing Adam's style applied inside a Kelvin thread*

The base voice governs tweet 1, 2, and 6 (hook, setup, close). Adam's
style governs the interior data tweets (3-5). These examples show what
that transition looks like in practice.

---

### Standalone Data Tweet (Adam mode, no thread)

Used when pushing a single on-chain observation to the timeline —
not attached to an episode, just a data moment worth surfacing.

```
there's more DeFi usage than the price action suggests

Aave crossed $22B in active borrows last week
highest since the 2021 cycle

the savers are here. the speculators just left.
```

> **What works:** Lowercase throughout. Counter-narrative hook first
> ("more than the price action suggests"). One number, plainly stated,
> on its own line. Source implicit in the chart embed. Close names the
> dynamic without moralizing — two short sentences that do opposite work.

---

### The Accumulation Stack (Adam mode, 3 consecutive tweets)

Used when three metrics all point at the same story. Three tweets,
not one. Each tweet has one line of text and one chart.

```
Tweet 1:
that's a lot of protocol revenue
[chart: Hyperliquid fees, $X this week]

Tweet 2:
and that's a lot of open interest
[chart: Hyperliquid OI, $X]

Tweet 3:
AND that's a lot of users doing it without a token subsidy
[chart: Hyperliquid DAUs vs. incentive programs = 0]
```

> **What works:** Parallel structure. Escalation only on the third beat
> ("AND" capitalized). Each chart carries the number — the text just
> names what we're looking at and why it's notable. The third tweet's
> extra clause ("without a token subsidy") is where the argument lives —
> but it lands harder because it's been earned by the stack.

---

### Data Tweet Inside a Post-Interview Thread (transition point)

Tweet 3 of a 6-tweet post-interview thread on a perp DEX founder.
Tweet 2 was in base voice (partner acknowledgment). Tweet 3 shifts to
Adam mode for the first data point.

```
[2/6] Firstly, before I get into the interview — shoutout to our partners
at BNB Chain for making this possible. It was a great time at Hanok House
with some incredible people in the space.

[3/6] the numbers behind why this conversation mattered right now:

$4.2B open interest on the platform at time of recording
up from $200M twelve months prior

[chart]
```

> **What works:** The shift from base voice (tweet 2) to Adam mode
> (tweet 3) is marked by the lowercase reset — "the numbers behind why
> this conversation mattered right now" signals the gear change without
> announcing it. The number stands alone. No hedging, no methodology note
> in the tweet itself.

## When Each Voice Does What

| Moment | Voice | Key Move |
|---|---|---|
| Hook / guest framing | Kelvin base | Warm, narrator, [N/total] |
| Partner acknowledgment | Kelvin base | Named, specific, genuine |
| First data point | Adam Tehc | Counter-narrative hook + one number |
| Second data point | Adam Tehc | Accumulation — parallel structure |
| Third data point / mechanism | Adam Tehc OR Santi/Haseeb | If data: Adam. If framework: Santi/Haseeb |
| Guest's surprising admission | Kelvin base | Connector verb + specific detail |
| Personal close / CTA | Kelvin base | Warm, understated, invitation |
